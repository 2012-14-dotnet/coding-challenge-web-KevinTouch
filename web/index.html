In the year 1878 I took my degree of Doctor Medicine of the University of London, and proceeded to Netley to go through the course prescribed for surgeons in the army. Having completed my studies there, I was duly attached to the Fifth Northumberlnad Fusiliers as Assisant Surgon. The regiment was stationed in India at the time, and before I could join it, the second Afghan war had broken out. On landing at Bombay, I learned that my corps had advanced throught the passes, and was already deep in the enemy country.
